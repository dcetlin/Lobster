Reconstruction starts from a prior model and fits the signal to it; decoding starts from the signal and reads its actual configuration. A reconstructed output would appear in substantially the same form from any Dan-like input profile. The trace goes back to the model, not to the session.

Pass 1 treated the operational work as substrate and the philosophical tension as the real thread. Dan doesn't separate them that cleanly.

Finally: "Named the hollow, moved to action" reads clean, but it softens the urgency in the voice note. There was fatigue there — not just diagnosis. A sharper pass would have held that rather than resolving it into a story about building.
