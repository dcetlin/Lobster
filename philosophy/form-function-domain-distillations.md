# Form-Function Domain Distillations

*v4 — April 26, 2026*

---

## Meta

Form earns trust by producing the same orientation regardless of who arrives at it or under what conditions — and loses it through drift so gradual each step looks like a reasonable local decision. The mark of a correctly-formed system is not that it is pleasant to navigate but that attention follows meaning directly: the mind moves through structure the way it moves through a familiar landscape, by recognition rather than decoding. Every element that forces a translation step is load-bearing in the wrong direction — it carries cognitive weight without adding conceptual structure.

---

## 1. Code / Functions

A function is correctly formed when its call site is the complete specification: what it requires, what it changes, what it returns are readable from the interface without opening the body. Every internal comment that explains what the code does — not why a tradeoff was made — marks a moment where the interface failed to carry its own weight; it is compensatory weight, not structural weight. Load-bearing elements compress information; compensatory elements expand it without adding structure. A correctly-formed function has none of the latter: the interface is not a summary of the body, it is the body's natural form at the level of its callers.

## 2. Architecture / System Design

A system is correctly designed when component names and boundaries make the dependency structure derivable without inference — and when organizational structure is isomorphic to conceptual structure at every scale: files, directories, namespaces, groupings. The names are where the concepts separate; the connections are where they depend; the groupings are where the concepts belong together. When a component's placement must be explained, or when a directory's contents can only be understood by someone who knows the system's history, the organizational layer is asserting a structure it does not actually have. Structure should be legible at the level it is encountered, not reconstructable from accumulated local knowledge.

## 3. Documents / Canon

A canonical document is correctly formed when the load-bearing fact arrives before reconstruction is required, and when the structure of the document mirrors the structure of what it is about — so that each level of abstraction is available without requiring the prior level to be held in working memory simultaneously. The failure is not inaccuracy — it is accurate information that can only be used after the reader rebuilds the structure the document failed to make visible. Each sentence that compensates for an earlier unclear sentence is evidence of drift; each explanatory layer added to patch a structural gap is overhead. The correction is to fix the structure, not to annotate it.

## 4. Concepts / Abstractions

A concept is correctly named when its boundary is carried by the name itself — not by a list of what it excludes. That list is the residue of a name pointing at a locally-convenient cluster rather than a real structure. The deeper failure is scaffolding: abstractions that require the lower level to be held in mind while working at the higher level have not been correctly formed — they force simultaneous multi-level awareness, which is the cognitive cost of bad scaffolding regardless of whether individual names are accurate. A correctly-formed abstraction makes the level below it available on demand but not mandatory in use. The name carries the shape; the shape carries the level; each level is a stable floor.

## 5. Oracle / Review Process

A verdict is correctly formed when the structural fact that determines the outcome appears first, and the evidence exists to support it. The failure modes are symmetric: a verdict without structural argument, and an observation catalog without determination — both require the reader to finish the work the review was supposed to complete. The distinction between load-bearing and compensatory applies directly here: observations are load-bearing when they support the determination; they are compensatory overhead when they substitute for it. A verdict whose determination must be inferred from a catalog of findings has the same structure as a document whose conclusion must be reconstructed from its evidence — the form is not carrying what it is for.

## 6. Collaboration / Communication

A reply is correctly formed when the fact that changes what the recipient does next appears in the first clause. The criterion is not whether a careful reader would find it — it is whether someone scanning under pressure, with reduced generosity and a specific next action pending, gets the essential thing before their attention moves on. This is ergonomics in the precise sense: not comfort but the property that attention follows meaning directly, without requiring the reader to decode the structure before using it. Burial past paragraph two is structural failure regardless of what is buried there. The reply that leads with context before signal has made the reader do the organizing work the sender was responsible for.

## 7. Organizational Structure

A structure is correctly organized when placement is derivable: the name signals the location and the location confirms the name, so that navigation becomes recognition rather than search. The criterion is not consistency alone — consistency produces predictability only when the underlying pattern language has already resolved to the right primitives, the right registers, the right groupings. Where those primitives are still contested or drifting, even a consistently applied scheme produces a system that must be learned rather than read. The failure mode is compensatory navigation: when a well-formed tool like search or an assistant substitutes for structural clarity that should have been present, the cognitive overhead has not been eliminated — it has been outsourced. A correctly-formed organizational structure moves in one direction: each hygiene decision, naming choice, and grouping resolves ambiguity rather than deferring it, so that the accumulation of small correct placements produces a whole where finding a thing confirms, rather than surprises, the person who finds it.
