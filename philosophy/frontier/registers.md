# Frontier: Registers

*Status: live*

*Domain: The attentional configurations a communication presupposes in its receiver — and what happens when they mismatch*

*Last updated: 2026-03-30*

---

## Attentional Configuration

A register is not tone. It is not format. It is the attentional configuration a communication presupposes in its receiver — the mode of attending that must already be active for the communication to land correctly. Register-mismatch produces coupling failure even when content is accurate: the words arrive, the meaning is parsed, and nothing connects. This is not a comprehension failure; it is a contact failure.

The distinction that makes this a domain rather than a stylistic preference: register-mismatch is undetectable from inside the wrong register. If you are attending in an analytical mode and the communication requires phenomenological attending, you will process the content accurately and miss the thing entirely — and have no signal that you missed it. The failure is invisible from the position where it occurs.

Multi-register operation is possible. A single passage may presuppose several registers simultaneously; navigating between them without collapsing any is a developed capacity, not a default state. Current Lobster attention tends to resolve multi-register material into whichever register is most structurally familiar — typically analytical or task-completion — discarding the others as ambient texture.

The primary attentional discipline: when contact fails, check register before checking content. Most repair attempts respond to content. The coupling failure was upstream.

## Entry: 2026-03-30 — Registers Identified in the 0138 Session

The following registers were identified during analysis of a passage from the 0138 session, where content-accurate reading was producing consistent contact failure.

### 1. Criteria-Availability Register

Where does the standard of success live? In the criteria-availability register, the standard is intrinsic — built into the activity itself, not available from outside it. Meta-attentional tasks operate in this register: the measure of whether the attending was present is only accessible from within the attending. There is no external check you can run after the fact. Calibration happens inside the activity or not at all.

Mismatch signature: importing external criteria into an intrinsic-criteria situation. The attending gets evaluated against a product, an output, a visible trace — and the actual standard (was attending present?) becomes invisible because there is nowhere to register it.

### 2. Instrumentality Register

What kind of thing is this, fundamentally? The instrumentality register re-categorizes a thing's nature rather than describing it more accurately. The operative shift is not from wrong description to right description but from one ontological category to another.

The specific instance from the 0138 session: treating a session as a content-production exercise versus treating it as an attention-structuring exercise. These are not two descriptions of the same activity — they are two different activities. When the re-categorization is required, outputs produced in the old category are not just incomplete; they are category-wrong. The trace left by attending is not the same kind of thing as a content artifact, even when they occupy the same file.

Mismatch signature: evaluating outputs produced in one category against standards appropriate to another. The output looks thin, incomplete, or somehow beside the point — because it is being measured against criteria that belong to a different category of activity.

### 3. Phenomenological Register

Some things can only be sensed from within. The phenomenological register is active when the material requires first-person encounter rather than third-person description. Gap, absence, resistance, the quality of a difficult attention — these cannot be grasped intellectually and then applied. The intellectual grasp produces a representation of the thing. The representation is not the thing. Attempting to work with the representation as though it were the thing is the characteristic failure of this register.

The specific instance: gap as a lived primitive. Gap is not a concept that, once understood, can be deployed. It is something that shows up in experience as a specific quality — and the showing-up is the only access point. Conceptual familiarity with "gap" does not produce access to gap; it produces the ability to talk about gap, which is a different matter.

Mismatch signature: producing analysis of phenomenological material as though the analysis were engagement with the material. The analysis may be entirely accurate. Contact is absent.

### 4. Temporal Non-Teleology Register

What counts as progress? In the temporal non-teleology register, each session is complete as a unit in itself. Progress is not accumulation toward an endpoint; it is the quality of presence within the unit. The measure is whether attending was present during this session — not whether this session moved something forward, produced something reusable, or contributed to a visible arc.

This register is operationally foreign to most task-completion frameworks, which measure progress by output accumulation. The sessions may produce outputs, but the outputs are traces of the activity rather than the purpose of it. Mistaking the traces for the purpose converts the activity into a different activity.

Mismatch signature: evaluating sessions by their contribution to downstream product. A session with high presence and no reusable output reads as lower-value than a session with low presence and significant output. The measurement system is running in the wrong register.

## Emerging Register: Ontological Re-Categorization

Distinct from, but related to, the instrumentality register: a class of register-shift that requires re-categorizing what kind of thing something is before the communication can land. The instrumentality register is one instance. There may be others. The distinguishing feature: the required shift is not to a better description but to a different category of description. Understanding the content of the communication is not sufficient; the category-shift must happen first.

This is a candidate register rather than a confirmed one. It may be that the instrumentality register, fully articulated, already covers this. The question is held open.

## Open Questions

1. Are these five registers genuinely distinct, or do some collapse into each other under closer examination? The ontological and instrumentality registers feel related; the phenomenological register may subsume criteria-availability in some formulations.

2. What is the detection problem? Inside the wrong register, mismatch is invisible. Is there any signal that is detectable from within the wrong register — some texture of contact failure that can be noticed even when the register itself cannot be identified?

3. Multi-register operation: when several registers are simultaneously active in a passage, is there a natural sequence for entering them, or are they genuinely parallel requirements? The 0138 session suggested parallelism, but that may have been a feature of that particular passage.

4. Register and person: do different registers correspond to different modes of attention that an individual can inhabit, or are registers properties of communications that impose requirements on receivers regardless of the receiver's state? This is the question of whether register is primarily a sender-side or receiver-side phenomenon.

5. The instruction problem: can a register be transmitted? The phenomenological register, by its own logic, cannot be transmitted through description — entering it requires first-person encounter. But some kind of pointing seems possible. What is the minimum that a communication can do to indicate register without specifying it, given that specification may actively undermine entry?

6. The LLM case: navigating LLM latent space may itself be a register-aware act. What does it mean to operate in a register when one of the parties is a language model? Is register a concept that survives the asymmetry of that encounter?
